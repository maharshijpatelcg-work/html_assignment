Masala Dosa

Masala Dosa is a famous South Indian dish made of a thin, crispy rice and lentil crepe filled with a spicy potato masala. It is usually served with coconut chutney and sambar. Light, tasty, and filling, it is loved across India and abroad.

    Ingredients:

  • 2 cups raw rice
  • ½ cup urad dal
  • 2 tbsp chana dal
  • ¼ tsp fenugreek seeds
  • 4-5 boiled potatoes
  • 2-3 green chilies
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • ¼ tsp turmeric powder
  • ½ tsp mustard seeds
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • Coconut chutney
  • Sambar

    Recipe (Simple Steps):

  1. Batter: Soak 2 cups rice and ½ cup urad dal overnight, grind into a smooth batter, and ferment for 8-10 hours.
  2. Potato Masala: Boil potatoes, mash them, and sauté with onions, green chilies, ginger, curry leaves, mustard seeds, and turmeric.
  3. Dosa: Heat a tawa (pan), pour a ladle of batter, and spread it thin. Drizzle little oil.
  4. Filling: Place the potato masala in the center, fold the dosa, and serve hot.
  5. Serve with: Coconut chutney and sambar.

Pasta

Pasta is a popular Italian dish made from wheat noodles, usually served with a flavorful sauce, vegetables, or meat. It's simple, filling, and can be customized to suit different tastes.

    Ingredients:

  • 2 cups pasta
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 2 tomatoes
  • 2-3 garlic cloves
  • 1 capsicum/bell pepper
  • 1-2 tsp olive oil or butter
  • ½ tsp chili flakes
  • ½ tsp oregano or Italian herbs
  • Grated cheese

    Simple Pasta Recipe:

  1. Boil Pasta: Cook 2 cups of pasta in salted boiling water until soft, then drain.
  2. Prepare Sauce: Heat oil/butter in a pan, sauté garlic and onions. Add tomatoes or ready-made pasta sauce, salt, chili flakes, and herbs (oregano/basil).
  3. Add Veggies/Meat (optional): Toss in cooked vegetables like capsicum, corn, or chicken.
  4. Mix: Add boiled pasta to the sauce and mix well.
  5. Serve: Garnish with cheese and serve hot.

Paneer Butter Masala

Paneer Butter Masala is a rich and creamy North Indian curry made with paneer (Indian cottage cheese), butter, and a spiced tomato-based gravy. It’s one of the most loved dishes in Indian restaurants, usually enjoyed with naan or rice.

    Ingridents :

  • 200-250g paneer
  • 2 tomatoes
  • 2-3 garlic cloves
  • 2-3 green chilies
  • 2-3 tbsp butter
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • ½ tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp coriander powder

Recipe (Simple Steps):

  1. Prepare Gravy: Heat butter, sauté onions, tomatoes, ginger, and garlic. Blend into a smooth paste.
  2. Cook Sauce: In the pan, add butter, the paste, salt, chili powder, garam masala, and cream.
  3. Add Paneer: Toss paneer cubes into the gravy and let it simmer for 5 minutes.
  4. Finish: Add a spoon of butter and a sprinkle of kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves).
  5. Serve: Enjoy hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice.
